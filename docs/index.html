My Teaching Philosophy

A Learner-Centered Approach

I believe that figuring out your students interests and incorporating them into your lesson plans, without coming off as a try-hard, is the best way to peek their interest and encourage them to participate in class. I also believe that a teacher shouldn't just be a person who stands in front of a black board but also life guide for his students, and a person they can come and talk to about things they wouldn't otherwise share with other people that is why respect and appreciation should be mutually communicated in class.

Growth Mindset

In Business, they say that you either earn or learn. I think that is applicable to every facet of life.

My Evaluation of the TESOL Training Program

I was reluctant to join the program in the beginning but it turned out to be a very nice experience. Here are some of the main pros and cons:

✅ Pros:

  • Good Trainers – This is the first time I encountered trainers who seemed to know their craft really well. They are also the best English teachers I've ever had.
  • Supportive Environment – Both trainers and peers were very nice and friendly. I never felt comfortable talking around people in any other situation like I did during this program.

❌ Cons:

  • Assignements – Some online assignements were repeated multiple times which confused me. Others didn't have correct answers listed for them when I went to check my scores.
